Who We Are

Building the Infrastructure India's Youth Deserve

"India allocates thousands of crores to sports — but millions of children never feel its impact. We exist to close that gap."

Kridati Sports Foundation is a Section 8 registered non-profit headquartered in Mumbai. We were founded with one conviction: the gap between government sports fund allocation and real, on-ground execution is a solvable problem — and we are the team to solve it.

We partner with schools, municipalities, sports federations, and corporate CSR programs to deliver end-to-end sports programming — starting with Football and Badminton, expanding into the full Olympic sports ecosystem.

  • Registered under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013
  • Empanelled under Sports Authority of India (SAI) framework
  • Aligned with Khelo India program delivery guidelines
  • CSR eligible under Schedule VII of the Companies Act
  • 12A & 80G registration in progress for tax-exempt donations

Government Partnership

Our Khelo India Execution Model

India's Khelo India program allocates hundreds of crores annually for grassroots sports. Kridati is purpose-built to bid for and execute these tenders — ensuring funds reach athletes, not just paperwork.

01

Tender Identification & Bidding

We systematically track Khelo India, SAI, and state sports tenders. Our team prepares compliant technical bids — coach CVs, venue tie-ups, equipment inventories, and full execution plans.

02

Ground-Level Execution

Unlike intermediaries who subcontract poorly, Kridati directly manages the event infrastructure — certified coaches, rented venues, equipment procurement, and day-to-day coordination.

03

Compliance & Reporting

We submit utilization certificates, attendance records, coach logs, and participant impact reports within government timelines — ensuring clean audits and repeat tender eligibility.

04

State-Level Expansion

Beachhead in Maharashtra, expanding to Haryana, Rajasthan, and UP. We build local government relationships through field research and district sports office engagement in each state.

Our Journey

Three-Year Execution Roadmap

A phased, capital-efficient plan from pilot to pan-India grassroots platform.

P1
Months
0 – 6

Phase 1 — Foundation & Pilot (Mumbai / Thane)

  • Complete Section 8 registration, SAI empanelment, and 12A/80G tax exemption filings
  • Conduct 3–5 community Football & Badminton tournaments in Thane and Kalyan districts
  • Onboard 8–10 freelance certified coaches on project-based contracts
  • Build school partnership network — target 15 schools in Thane / Mumbai region
  • Launch social media presence and Kridati brand identity across platforms
  • Submit first Khelo India district-level tender in Maharashtra
Budget: ₹5–6 Lakhs · CSR + Local Sponsors
P2
Months
6 – 18

Phase 2 — Corporate Expansion & Khelo India Entry

  • Launch corporate sports event packages; target 5 corporate clients in Mumbai MMR
  • Organize seasonal inter-school leagues — Football, Badminton, Athletics
  • Win and execute first Khelo India state-level training camp tender
  • Establish Haryana operations — field research, district sports office meetings, partnerships
  • Expand coach network to 25+ certified coaches across Maharashtra and Haryana
  • Launch Kridati Talent Hunt (first edition) — refer 20+ athletes to SAI academies
Budget: ₹15–25 Lakhs · Tender Revenue + Corporate Fees + Grants
P3
Months
18 – 36

Phase 3 — Olympic Sports Integration & Multi-State Scale

  • Launch 5-day intensive camps for 4+ Olympic disciplines: Athletics, Judo, Boxing, Wrestling
  • Host annual Kridati Multi-Sport Grassroots Olympics Festival (flagship event)
  • Expand to Pune, Navi Mumbai, and Haryana zonal hubs with local partners
  • Achieve operational self-sufficiency via tender revenue, corporate fees, and CSR income
  • Lay groundwork for Kridati Olympic Academy — full-time athlete training with school partners
Budget: ₹50–80 Lakhs · Multi-Stream · Investor-Backed
